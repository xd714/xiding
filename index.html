About

I develop computational methods for animal breeding and genetics at FBN Germany. My work focuses on statistical modeling, machine learning, and genomic analysis to improve livestock breeding efficiency.

My journey started at China Agricultural University, led me through an exchange at University of Wisconsin, and brought me to Germany for my Master's at University of Rostock. After industry experience at KWS LOCHOW in plant breeding, I returned to research for my PhD at FBN.

I combine statistical modeling, machine learning, and genomic analysis to solve real problems in animal breeding, always with an eye toward sustainability and global impact.
