About

I apply computational methods for animal breeding and genetics at FBN Germany, working with statistical modelling, machine learning, and genomic analysis to address real challenges in livestock production.

My path started at China Agricultural University, continued through an exchange at the University of Wisconsin, and brought me to Germany for a Master’s at the University of Rostock. After industry experience at KWS LOCHOW in plant breeding, I returned to research for my PhD at FBN — completing April 2026.

Across research and industry, I am drawn to the intersection of quantitative genetics and data science, where biological questions meet computational solutions, with an eye toward sustainable food production and global impact.
